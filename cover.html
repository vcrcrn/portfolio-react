About Me


My name is Victor Cornejo and I'm a Cognitive and Data Scientist. I reside in the San Francisco Bay area and recently graduted from The University of California, Berkeley.

Over time I have accumulated diverse life experiences and a critical approach to seeing the world around me. In contrast, academia has taught me to be analytical, evidence-based, and data-oriented. I incorporate both aspects into my life philosophy.

In an era where reality has become detached from objectivity, understanding our ontological position becomes imperative. Artificial intelligence poses new challenges as to what it means to be human, while decoding our brain's black box makes us question the degree to which we can control our decisions as individuals. Under this context, current social challenges are dependent on the flow of information.

My goal is to make 'things' approachable and accessible. I believe knowledge should be accessible for everyone, and that design should be intuitive by their intended audience. Moreover, I aim to incorporate scientific knowledge into the mainstream and public sector areas. I believe this is possible in a ludic way with the help of citizen science, and most importantly, from data transparency.

I'm also a lab assistant for the Whitney Lab for Perception and Action at UC Berkeley. In the future, I want to continue pursuing research. My major interest lies on Emotion in Visual Perception and its Cross-cultural Dependency. In the mean time, I aim to work with entities whose mission is to improve the human condition through science, education, and ethics, while questioning the existing values of the latter.


My Foundations

Sciences


Our reasoning abilities as complex animals depend on our neuro-biological configuration and its interaction in our constantly changing environment. Data sciences can help us predict and optimize behaviors at the macro level outside of a deterministic view.


Humanities


Philosophy and Ethics are both disciplines that I consider essential to understanding the implications of our nature across diverse paradigms and cultures, allowing for analysis of how values shape our perception of the world, from our cognitive assemblage to society and its norms.


Design


A theory is nothing without practice, and practice is nothing without a theory. I can identify patterns and actors to design solutions and implement them accordingly. From simple aesthetic trends to the research of user mediated interactions.
