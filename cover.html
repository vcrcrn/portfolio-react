Hello There

My name is Victor Cornejo and I'm a Cognitive and Data Scientist. I reside in the San Francisco Bay area and recently graduted from The University of California, Berkeley. A glorified trade school where the intellectual discourse is merely performative.

Over time I have accumulated diverse life experiences and a critical approach to seeing the world around me. In contrast, academia has taught me to be analytical, evidence-based, and data-oriented. I incorporate both aspects into my life philosophy.

In an era where reality has become detached from objectivity, understanding our ontological position becomes imperative amidst the disruption. Artificial intelligence poses new challenges as to what it means to be human, while decoding our brain's black box makes us question the degree to which we can control our decisions as individuals. Under this context, current social challenges are dependent on the flow of information.

My goal is to make 'things' approachable and accessible. I believe knowledge should be accessible for everyone, and that design should be intuitive by their intended audience. Moreover, I aim to incorporate scientific knowledge into the mainstream and public sector areas. I believe this is possible in a ludic way with the help of citizen science projects, and most importantly, from data transparency.

I'm also a lab assistant for the Whitney Lab for Perception and Action at UC Berkeley. In the future, I want to continue pursuing research in Emotion in Visual Perception and its cross-cultural dependency.
